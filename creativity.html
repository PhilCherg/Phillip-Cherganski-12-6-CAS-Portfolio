Creativity

Robotics, piano, websites, and more...

Major creative experiences in my senior year have been designing and programming a 3D-printed robot car, playing the piano, and performing at Concerts. While I primarily focus on these in my portfolio entries below, I have also created a mobile game using Unity and a checklist web service using C# and ASP.NET. Overall, I have improved my designing, programming, and piano skills as a result of my creative efforts this year.

STEM Robotics Club Introductions

Semester 1, 2019

Here begins this year's journey of the STEM Robotics Club at ACS. Being the lector of the club, I introduced the new members to the club’s goals and tools.

Figuring out how to best explain key topics, such as the basics of 3D-modelling, programming in Python, and working with the various equipment we had at our disposal in SCIFI, was a creative endeavor in its own right. It was fun coming up with different ways of conveying my skills and experience in the field to newcomers in a way that was informative, but also easy to understand and engaging. I decided that the best course of action would be to complete the same tasks as the students. This would not only give me a better understanding of their point of view, but also allow me to create my own model in the process.

I started by brainstorming ideas for a project with the students. After everyone settled on creating a 3D-printed car, I asked them for design ideas, so that I could get some inspiration, but also to grasp their level of creativity and abilities. Following some extensive brainstorming, it was time for me to get to work designing my own prototype.

STEM Robotics Club Model Design and 3D-Printing

December 4, 2019

After all the students at the club decided on designing, printing, and programming a 3D-printed car, it was time for me to teach them how to effectively use SketchUp. Apart from showing them how to use SketchUp to create their own models, which I focus on in the Service section of this portfolio, I also decided to design my own prototype. This was a creative way for me to refresh my own SketchUp skills, and it also allowed me to create a robot car myself.

I brainstormed a couple of ideas, made a few test sketches, and eventually ended up with a prototype chassis. After all the designing was done, I 3D-printed it in SCIFI. I experimented with various options, such as print precision and density, to test the stability of the model, since ensuring the chassis’ integrity of the car is essential for producing a working model that does not easily break when exposed to tension, such as ground friction or the rotating motors.

STEM Robotics Club Programming in Python

December 11, 2019

Designing the model in SketchUp was not such a lengthy process, and it took me a couple of days to complete. However, printing 5 different models with various combinations of precision and density took more time. Since the prints took around 7 hours to finish, I would just start them when I got to school and pick them up at the end of the day.

Then, I equipped the best model with the necessary hardware. There were some Arduino sets at our disposal, which included a motherboard, motors, wheels, etc. I put all of them together and tested them out. I decided to install all the wiring last, since having connected cables all over the place would make moving parts and changing the layout very hard.

Finally, I started programming the robot using Python. This was a new experience, since I had previously only programmed LEGO Mindstorms sets using Python. Connecting the Arduino set and figuring out how to control it was a cool challenge for me to overtake. It took some time and research online, but at the end, I got it all working.

Piano Lessons

2 hours a week

I have been playing the piano for 8 years now. I started out with an organized plan, where I followed a textbook of simple practice piano pieces to learn the keys and develop my technique. This went on for a while, and I was able to learn to play beginner and intermediate pieces relatively well. However, I didn't feel like most of the pieces resonated with me, since they were written for training purposes and not to sound melodically exceptional.

At ACS, I signed up for the ACS Instrumental Studio in my Prep year. Not long after, I started working with a new musical coach, and I have been having piano lessons for 2 hours each week ever since.

The current approach is significantly different than the one before. It is professional yet relaxed. Whenever we are about to start a new piece, we discuss various styles that would be of interest to me. We listen to some classical and contemporary pieces, and I chose the ones I like. I have been learning to play music that sounds amazing, and I enjoy my piano lessons very much.

Christmas Concert "The Dark Knight"

December 15, 2019

Throughout the years, I have learnt to play a number of classical and contemporary pieces. My teacher holds the tradition of organizing Christmas and summer concerts, where all of his students can perform in front of their parents, family, and friends what they have been working on during the year. Those are really great events, since you get to play in front of a familiar audience, without having too much stress.

For this year's Christmas concert, I had been practicing a piece called "The Dark Knight," which is a 10-minute collection of all the major themes of the movie "Batman: The Dark Knight," written by the composer Hans Zimmer. I really enjoyed learning and playing this piece in my free time, since it sounds epic from start to finish.

The concert was a great success for me. Everyone enjoyed my performance of "The Dark Knight." Throughout the years, I have come to know some of the students' parents. They have been observing my progress and always feel very impressed by my performances. Having this appreciation from someone outside my immediate family is a really refreshing and motivating feeling.

ACS Classical Concert

March 25, 2020 (Cancelled)

I have been part of the ACS Instrumental studio since my Prep year. Each week, I practice at school and learn to play a classical piece for the annual ASC Classical Concert. Being able to practice at school and get feedback and tutoring from yet another teacher is really useful, since I get a taste of a different teaching style and receive additional advice and assistance.

This year, I had a hard time coming up with a piece to perform at the Classical Concert. Since I am a Senior, I wanted to perform something memorable for my last ever ASC Classical Concert. After some brainstorming with my piano teacher, I decided to play "O Fortuna" by Carl Orff. It is both a well-known classical piece, and it also sounds grand, so it was sure to attract the attention of the audience and leave everyone impressed.

Learning the piece was a very enjoyable process, since I liked playing it often in my free time, purely because of how epic it sounded. I feel like I have mastered it by now. However, due to the flu vacation, the Auditions and the concert have been cancelled. While this is a bummer, I am still practicing "O Fortuna" from time to time, both to refresh my abilities, and also to brighten my mood.
